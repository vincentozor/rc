God of War - Origins Collection Review

Released in September 2011, the God of War - Origins Collection brought two prequel titles from the acclaimed God of War series to the PlayStation 3. This compilation includes remastered versions of God of War: Chains of Olympus and God of War: Ghost of Sparta, which originally debuted on the PlayStation Portable (PSP). Developed by Ready at Dawn and Santa Monica Studio, the collection offers a chance to experience Kratos' origin story with enhanced visuals and improved performance. In this review, we will examine the gameplay, story, technical enhancements, and overall experience of God of War - Origins Collection on the PS3.

Introduction

God of War - Origins Collection serves as an excellent way for fans to revisit the beginnings of Kratos’ journey. With both games having previously been exclusive to the PSP, the collection offers enhanced graphics, smoother gameplay, and the opportunity to experience the entire story arc on the PlayStation 3. The collection was released as part of Sony’s initiative to bring its popular handheld games to its more powerful home console. This review covers the merits of the collection in both the gameplay and technical aspects.

Gameplay

The gameplay in God of War - Origins Collection closely mirrors that of the mainline games in the franchise. Both Chains of Olympus and Ghost of Sparta maintain the core mechanics of brutal combat, puzzle-solving, and platforming that have made the series a hallmark of action-adventure games.

  • Combat Mechanics: The combat in both games is fast-paced and fluid. Kratos uses a combination of powerful weapons, such as the Blades of Chaos, and magic abilities to defeat enemies. The game maintains the series' signature combat system, which includes quick-time events (QTEs) and brutal finishing moves.
  • Platforming and Puzzle-Solving: As with other titles in the franchise, both games feature sections requiring precision platforming and puzzle-solving. The puzzles are designed to challenge the player’s logic and ability to interact with the environment while breaking up the combat-heavy sequences.
  • Upgrade System: Both games feature an upgrade system that allows Kratos to enhance his weapons, magic abilities, and health. The upgrade system is rewarding and encourages players to explore each level for hidden items and secrets.
  • Enemies and Boss Fights: The games feature a variety of enemies, each with unique behaviors and attack patterns. Boss fights in both titles are some of the most memorable moments, requiring quick reflexes and strategy to defeat the game’s massive foes.

Both games in the Origins Collection retain the brutal, visceral gameplay that fans of the God of War franchise have come to expect. The fast-paced combat, intricate platforming, and satisfying puzzle-solving work together to deliver an engaging experience that holds up even years after its original release on the PSP.

Story and Narrative

The narrative in God of War - Origins Collection provides a deeper look into Kratos’ backstory, further exploring the events that shaped him into the vengeful character seen in the mainline games. The collection presents two distinct chapters in Kratos’ journey, each exploring different facets of his past.

  • God of War: Chains of Olympus: Set before the events of the first game, Chains of Olympus follows Kratos as he is tasked with saving the gods of Olympus from the ancient Persian god, Morpheus. This game focuses on Kratos’ servitude to the gods, detailing his struggles with the consequences of his actions.
  • God of War: Ghost of Sparta: Taking place after the events of God of War, Ghost of Sparta explores Kratos’ search for his long-lost brother, Deimos. The game delves into Kratos’ guilt over his family’s death and his quest for redemption. This installment provides critical insight into Kratos’ motivations and personal struggles.

The narrative in the Origins Collection is emotionally compelling, as it touches on themes of vengeance, loss, and the search for redemption. Both games explore the complexity of Kratos’ character, offering players a deeper understanding of his troubled past. The strong narrative, combined with the series’ signature cutscenes and voice acting, makes these two prequels an essential part of the God of War story.

Technical Enhancements

The Origins Collection enhances both Chains of Olympus and Ghost of Sparta with improved graphics and smoother gameplay, taking full advantage of the PS3 hardware. While both games were originally designed for the PSP’s smaller screen and limited capabilities, the remastered versions on the PS3 deliver a visually stunning experience.

  • HD Remaster: The games are presented in full 720p resolution, offering much sharper textures, more detailed environments, and improved lighting compared to the PSP versions. The visuals benefit from the increased power of the PS3, making the world feel more immersive and polished.
  • Smoother Frame Rate: One of the most notable improvements in the collection is the smoother frame rate. Both games run at a consistent 30 frames per second, providing a much more fluid and enjoyable gameplay experience, especially during the fast-paced combat and intense action sequences.
  • Improved Audio: The sound design in the collection benefits from the PS3’s more powerful audio hardware, providing a richer experience with enhanced sound effects and voice acting. The orchestral soundtrack is crisp and adds to the epic scale of the game.

These technical improvements make a significant difference, elevating the overall experience and ensuring that the games hold up well against their more recent counterparts in the series. The transition from the PSP to PS3 is seamless, with the remaster providing a polished version of the original handheld titles.

Controls and User Interface

As the games were originally designed for the PSP, the control scheme has been adapted to the PlayStation 3’s DualShock 3 controller. The controls in the Origins Collection are intuitive, making it easy for players to jump straight into the action.

  • Combat Controls: The combat controls have been optimized for the PS3, with responsive button inputs that make Kratos’ attacks and combos feel fluid. The game retains its iconic light and heavy attacks, as well as the quick-time events that are a hallmark of the series.
  • Platforming and Puzzle Controls: The platforming controls are precise, making the more challenging sequences manageable. The puzzles, while varied, are intuitive and require players to think logically about how to manipulate the environment.
  • Camera Controls: The camera system works well, allowing players to adjust the viewpoint with ease. The camera’s movement is smooth, making it easy to survey the environment and locate enemies or items.

The controls feel natural and responsive, with no major issues or complaints. The transition from the PSP to PS3 has been handled well, ensuring that the gameplay experience remains seamless and enjoyable.

Strengths

The God of War - Origins Collection has several strengths that make it an essential addition for fans of the series:

  • Enhanced HD visuals and smoother performance, making the games feel fresh and modern.
  • Two compelling prequels that provide valuable backstory and character development for Kratos.
  • Intense, fluid combat that stays true to the core God of War experience.
  • Rich narrative and strong voice acting that immerse players in the world of Greek mythology.
  • Intuitive controls and a seamless transition from PSP to PS3 gameplay.

Weaknesses

Despite the numerous strengths, the collection does have some weaknesses:

  • Shorter Playtime: Both games are relatively short compared to other titles in the God of War franchise, making the collection feel less substantial.
  • Lack of New Content: While the games have been remastered, there are no new levels, features, or substantial additions to the original PSP releases.

These weaknesses are minor and do not significantly detract from the overall experience, especially for players looking to dive deeper into Kratos’ backstory.

Conclusion

God of War - Origins Collection is an excellent compilation that allows players to experience the origins of Kratos on the PlayStation 3. The remastered graphics, smoother gameplay, and engaging storylines make these two prequels an essential part of the God of War series. While the collection may be shorter than some fans would prefer, it offers an opportunity to dive deeper into Kratos’ backstory and adds value to the franchise’s overall narrative.

For both longtime fans and newcomers, God of War - Origins Collection is a must-play. It successfully translates the PSP experience to the PS3, making it a worthwhile addition to any fan’s collection.
