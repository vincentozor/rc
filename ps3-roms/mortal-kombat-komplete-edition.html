Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition Review

Released in 2012, Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition is the definitive version of the critically acclaimed Mortal Kombat reboot, originally launched in 2011. Developed by NetherRealm Studios and published by Warner Bros. Interactive Entertainment, this edition compiles the original game along with all downloadable content (DLC), additional characters, and stages, offering a complete and enhanced experience for fans of the series. Featuring an array of new characters, improved gameplay mechanics, and a deeper narrative, Komplete Edition builds upon its predecessor to deliver a comprehensive fighting game experience. This review explores the various features of the game, including the expanded roster, revamped gameplay, and additional content, providing an in-depth look at what makes Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition a standout title in the franchise.

Introduction

Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition is an upgrade to the 2011 reboot of the classic franchise. The Komplete Edition features all the DLC content available for the original game, including new characters, skins, and stages. Released for multiple platforms including PlayStation 3, Xbox 360, and PC, this edition was a must-have for players who wanted the full experience. The 2011 Mortal Kombat reboot marked a return to the series' roots, offering traditional 2D fighting mechanics while incorporating new features like a deep story mode, more interactive environments, and brutal finishing moves that the series is known for. The Komplete Edition takes this to the next level, providing fans with all the content they could ever want in one package.

Story Mode

The story mode in Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition is one of its standout features. It is set in the timeline after the events of Mortal Kombat: Armageddon and follows the struggle for control over Earthrealm as various factions—such as the forces of Earthrealm, Outworld, and the Netherrealm—battle for dominance. The story is set in a way that allows players to experience the events of the previous games through the eyes of different characters, using their unique fighting styles and abilities. It is an action-packed narrative that includes cinematic cutscenes, quick-time events, and, of course, epic battles.

Plot and Characters

The plot of Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition revolves around the machinations of the evil sorcerer Shang Tsung, who conspires with other villains such as Shao Kahn and Quan Chi to take control of the realms. Players are introduced to a host of iconic characters from the Mortal Kombat universe, including Liu Kang, Scorpion, Sub-Zero, Raiden, Johnny Cage, and others. The story mode seamlessly integrates these characters into the narrative, allowing players to take control of them during key moments in the story.

The plot itself is filled with drama and intrigue, with characters' motivations and allegiances evolving throughout the story. Raiden, for example, is portrayed as a more conflicted character than in previous titles, torn between his desire to protect Earthrealm and his understanding of the greater consequences of his actions. This depth in character development makes the story more than just a series of battles—it's an emotional journey that explores themes of sacrifice, power, and betrayal.

Mission Variety

The story mode in Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition consists of a series of chapters, each focusing on a different character. Each chapter offers a mix of fights, interactive sequences, and cinematic cutscenes. The missions are varied in their objectives, from simple one-on-one battles to multi-fighter confrontations, making each chapter feel fresh and exciting. The final battle of the story mode is a climactic showdown that tests everything players have learned up to that point, offering a rewarding conclusion to the campaign.

  • One-on-One Fights: These are the classic Mortal Kombat-style battles between two characters.
  • Tag-Team Fights: Players can control two characters in tag-team matches, adding an extra layer of strategy.
  • Quick-Time Events: These events require players to press certain buttons during key story moments to succeed.

Gameplay and Mechanics

Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition builds upon the foundation set by the original 2011 reboot, improving upon the core mechanics to create a faster, more fluid experience. The game retains its 2D fighting mechanics, which was a deliberate return to the series' roots after the 3D entries in earlier installments. However, it incorporates modern design elements such as interactive environments, combo systems, and various meter-based mechanics that add depth and strategy to the gameplay.

Combat System

The combat system in Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition is highly technical and relies heavily on combo-based mechanics. Players can perform a wide variety of attacks, including basic strikes, special moves, and devastating combos. The game includes a comprehensive tutorial mode that allows new players to learn the basics of combat while also offering advanced techniques for more seasoned players.

One of the key features of the combat system is the inclusion of the "X-Ray" move, a brutal attack that allows players to deal massive damage while simultaneously showcasing a graphic display of broken bones and internal injuries. These X-Ray moves add an extra layer of excitement to each battle and encourage players to learn how to strategically use their meter to unleash these powerful attacks.

Character Roster

The Komplete Edition features an expanded roster of characters, with all of the downloadable characters from the original game included. This brings the total number of playable fighters to over 30, including iconic characters such as Scorpion, Sub-Zero, Raiden, and Sonya Blade, as well as new additions like Kratos from the God of War series, Freddy Krueger from the A Nightmare on Elm Street franchise, and more. The inclusion of these characters gives players even more options to choose from, each with their unique fighting styles and moves.

  • Scorpion: A classic character known for his teleportation abilities and fiery attacks.
  • Sub-Zero: A fan favorite who specializes in ice-based attacks and defensive techniques.
  • Kratos: The protagonist of God of War, bringing his brutal combat style and iconic weapons to the fight.

Additional Content

In addition to the base game and story mode, Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition includes a wealth of additional content. This includes new skins, fatalities, and stages, all of which add to the overall depth of the game. The DLC content that was originally available for purchase has been bundled into the Komplete Edition, ensuring that players get the full experience without needing to buy additional content separately.

Graphics and Audio

For its time, Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition boasts impressive visuals, showcasing detailed character models, fluid animations, and highly detailed stages. The game’s graphics capture the brutal, over-the-top nature of the franchise while also providing a level of realism that enhances the overall experience. The environments are dynamic, with interactive elements that allow players to interact with their surroundings during combat.

Sound Design

The sound design in Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition is equally impressive. The game features a heavy, pulse-pounding soundtrack that complements the high-intensity nature of the gameplay. The sound effects for punches, kicks, and special moves are all satisfying, with bone-crushing noises and explosive effects enhancing the combat. The voice acting is also top-notch, with iconic characters such as Raiden and Scorpion delivering memorable lines. The sound design works in tandem with the visuals to create an immersive atmosphere that draws players into the game.

Multiplayer Mode

The multiplayer component of Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition allows players to test their skills against others online. The game features a variety of modes, including ranked matches, player matches, and the King of the Hill mode, where players can compete for dominance in a dynamic, ever-changing environment. The multiplayer experience is fast-paced and challenging, requiring players to master the characters and combos to succeed. Online play also includes the ability to earn rewards and achievements, encouraging players to keep competing and improving their skills.

Conclusion

Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition is the ultimate version of the 2011 reboot, offering everything fans loved about the original game along with a host of additional content that enhances the experience. The expanded roster, improved combat system, and additional skins, fatalities, and stages make this edition a must-have for fans of the franchise. Whether you're a long-time fan of Mortal Kombat or a newcomer to the series, Komplete Edition delivers a highly polished, action-packed experience that will keep you coming back for more.

With its engaging story mode, deep gameplay mechanics, and robust multiplayer options, Mortal Kombat: Komplete Edition is an essential entry in the Mortal Kombat franchise. It brings together the best elements of the series and adds more content, making it the definitive edition for players looking for a complete and satisfying fighting game experience.
