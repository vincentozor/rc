WWE 2K17 - A Comprehensive Review

WWE 2K17, developed by Yuke’s and Visual Concepts and published by 2K Sports, is a professional wrestling video game that offers fans an immersive WWE experience. Released in October 2016 for various platforms, including the PlayStation 3, the game serves as the 18th installment in the WWE video game series. With its extensive roster, enhanced gameplay mechanics, and a variety of modes, WWE 2K17 aims to cater to both casual players and die-hard wrestling enthusiasts.

Introduction

WWE 2K17 builds upon its predecessors by refining gameplay, expanding its features, and delivering an authentic WWE experience. This review delves into the game’s key elements, such as its roster, gameplay mechanics, modes, and presentation, to determine how it stacks up in the long-running franchise.

Roster and Character Models

One of the standout features of WWE 2K17 is its extensive roster, which includes current WWE superstars, legends, and NXT talent.

  • Extensive Roster: Over 130 wrestlers are available, featuring top stars like Brock Lesnar (the cover athlete), John Cena, and Roman Reigns, as well as iconic legends like The Rock and Stone Cold Steve Austin.
  • NXT Representation: Rising stars from WWE’s developmental brand, such as Shinsuke Nakamura and Asuka, add variety to the roster.
  • Realistic Models: Wrestler likenesses are accurately captured, though some models on the PS3 version show limitations due to hardware constraints.

The diversity and authenticity of the roster ensure that fans can find their favorite superstars and create dream matches.

Gameplay Mechanics

WWE 2K17 introduces refinements to its gameplay mechanics, enhancing the overall wrestling experience.

  • Improved Controls: The simplified control scheme makes the game accessible to newcomers while retaining depth for seasoned players.
  • Reversal System: The game features a stamina-based reversal mechanic, requiring strategic timing and planning.
  • New Grappling System: The addition of contextual grapples allows for more dynamic and fluid wrestling moves.
  • Increased Match Variety: Players can engage in ladder matches, Hell in a Cell, and backstage brawls, among others.

The gameplay strikes a balance between simulation and arcade elements, appealing to a broad audience.

Modes and Features

The game offers a range of modes that cater to different playstyles and preferences.

  • MyCareer Mode: Players can create a custom wrestler and guide them through a career in WWE, featuring rivalries, promos, and championships.
  • Universe Mode: This sandbox mode allows players to control the WWE universe, including storylines, rosters, and events.
  • Creation Suite: The robust creation tools enable players to design custom wrestlers, arenas, entrances, and championship belts.
  • Exhibition Matches: A quick-play mode for fans who want to jump straight into the action with their favorite wrestlers.

The variety of modes ensures there is something for every type of player, whether they enjoy storytelling, competition, or creativity.

Graphics and Presentation

The presentation in WWE 2K17 is a mixed bag on the PlayStation 3.

  • Character Models: While most superstars are faithfully recreated, some textures and animations appear dated due to hardware limitations.
  • Arenas and Crowds: Authentic arenas and enthusiastic crowd reactions enhance the atmosphere, but the visuals lack polish compared to newer platforms.
  • Entrances: The detailed entrances, complete with signature moves and pyrotechnics, contribute to the authenticity.

Despite its graphical shortcomings, the game captures the energy and spectacle of WWE events.

Audio Design

The audio in WWE 2K17 plays a significant role in immersing players in the wrestling experience.

  • Commentary: The commentary team provides play-by-play analysis, though it can feel repetitive over time.
  • Soundtrack: Curated by Diddy, the licensed soundtrack includes a mix of genres that complement the game’s vibe.
  • Wrestler Voices: Promos and voiceovers from superstars add to the realism.
  • Sound Effects: The impact of strikes, slams, and crowd reactions are convincingly portrayed.

The audio design enhances the game’s atmosphere, making matches feel like live WWE broadcasts.

Technical Performance

The PlayStation 3 version of WWE 2K17 demonstrates both strengths and limitations.

  • Stable Frame Rate: The game runs smoothly in most situations, ensuring a consistent experience.
  • Loading Times: Lengthy loading screens can interrupt the flow of gameplay.
  • Physics and Animations: The physics system occasionally results in awkward animations or glitches.

While the performance is serviceable, the aging hardware shows its limitations when compared to current-generation consoles.

Backstage Brawls

One of the standout features in WWE 2K17 is the return of backstage brawls. Players can take the action out of the ring and into the backstage area, utilizing various objects and environments to gain an advantage. This feature adds variety and excitement to matches, breaking the monotony of traditional bouts.

Strengths

WWE 2K17 has several standout features that contribute to its appeal:

  • Extensive and diverse roster.
  • Engaging gameplay mechanics with strategic depth.
  • Variety of modes, including MyCareer and Universe.
  • Robust creation tools for customization.
  • Authentic WWE presentation and atmosphere.

Weaknesses

Despite its strengths, the game is not without flaws:

  • Dated graphics and textures on the PlayStation 3 version.
  • Repetitive commentary during matches.
  • Occasional glitches and awkward animations.
  • Lengthy loading times.

These issues, while noticeable, do not significantly detract from the overall experience.

Critical Reception

WWE 2K17 received mixed to positive reviews from critics and fans:

  • Praise: The extensive roster, engaging gameplay, and variety of modes were widely praised.
  • Criticism: The lack of a 2K Showcase mode and graphical limitations on older platforms were common points of critique.

The game was generally regarded as a solid entry in the WWE franchise, despite its shortcomings.

Legacy and Replayability

WWE 2K17 remains a notable installment in the series, particularly for its emphasis on customization and freedom. The extensive creation suite and variety of modes ensure players can continue to enjoy the game long after completing its main features. For fans of professional wrestling, the game offers a fulfilling experience that captures the spirit of WWE.

Conclusion

WWE 2K17 for the PlayStation 3 is a solid wrestling game that delivers an authentic WWE experience. While its graphics and technical performance are limited by the aging hardware, the game excels in gameplay, roster depth, and customization options. Whether you are a casual fan or a dedicated wrestling enthusiast, WWE 2K17 offers plenty of content and excitement to keep you entertained.
