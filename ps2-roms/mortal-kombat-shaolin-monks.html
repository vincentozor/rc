Mortal Kombat - Shaolin Monks Gameplay

Unlike its predecessors in the Mortal Kombat franchise, which were primarily focused on one-on-one fighting, Shaolin Monks adopts an action-adventure format, combining free-roaming exploration with the series’ signature martial arts combat. The game follows Liu Kang and Kung Lao as they venture through a series of levels, battling enemies, solving puzzles, and engaging in dynamic combat encounters. While the core of the game is built around fighting, there is also a substantial emphasis on platforming, exploration, and cooperation.

The combat system in Shaolin Monks is a seamless blend of traditional Mortal Kombat mechanics with a more fluid, combo-based approach. Players can unleash powerful combos, perform iconic Mortal Kombat finishing moves, and utilize special attacks that are familiar to fans of the series. The game also introduces cooperative gameplay, allowing two players to team up and engage in combat together, which enhances the experience, especially in boss fights and large enemy mobs.

Key gameplay features include:

  • Combo System: The combo system is accessible yet rewarding, with simple button combinations that can be chained together to perform devastating attacks.
  • Special Moves: Players can execute signature Mortal Kombat moves, such as Liu Kang’s Bicycle Kick and Kung Lao’s Hat Toss, adding familiarity and fan service to the game.
  • Fatalities and Brutalities: As with any Mortal Kombat title, finishing moves are a hallmark of the gameplay. Players can perform Fatalities, Brutalities, and other graphic finishing moves after defeating an opponent.
  • Exploration and Puzzle-Solving: While combat remains the focal point, the game features exploration elements that require players to solve puzzles and unlock new paths to progress through levels.
  • Co-op Multiplayer: The cooperative mode allows two players to team up and play through the campaign, with combined moves and special attacks available during certain combat scenarios.

The game is structured in a series of stages that follow a linear progression, though each area is vast enough to provide multiple challenges and rewards. As players advance through the game, they encounter a variety of enemies, from common foot soldiers to powerful bosses that require strategy and skill to defeat. The combat is fast-paced, with an emphasis on fluidity and chaining attacks together, making it one of the most engaging aspects of the game.

Story: Expanding the Mortal Kombat Universe

The story of Mortal Kombat - Shaolin Monks takes place during the events of the original Mortal Kombat tournament, exploring the backstory of two of the series’ most iconic characters—Liu Kang and Kung Lao. The narrative focuses on their journey to thwart the evil sorcerer Shang Tsung and the powerful warlord Quan Chi, who are attempting to take control of the Mortal Kombat tournament and bring chaos to the realms.

The plot provides a fresh perspective on the events of the Mortal Kombat universe, allowing players to experience key moments in the series from the viewpoint of Liu Kang and Kung Lao. As the game progresses, players uncover various twists and turns, including encounters with familiar characters such as Scorpion, Sub-Zero, and Raiden. This story-driven approach adds depth to the Mortal Kombat universe, providing a backstory for many of the franchise's iconic characters and events.

The narrative is told through in-game cutscenes and dialogue, which are effective in advancing the story while maintaining the franchise’s signature tone of dark humor and intense drama. Although the plot is not as deep or intricate as some other action-adventure games, it serves its purpose in providing context for the game’s events and driving the action forward.

Character Roster: Familiar Faces and New Additions

As expected from any Mortal Kombat title, Shaolin Monks features a roster of familiar characters from the franchise, each with their own unique fighting styles and special moves. Liu Kang and Kung Lao serve as the primary protagonists, but players also encounter a wide range of antagonists and supporting characters throughout the game.

The playable characters include:

  • Kung Lao: The second protagonist, distinguished by his hat-based attacks and strong close-combat abilities.
  • Scorpion: A fan-favorite, Scorpion is a formidable fighter who specializes in teleportation and fiery attacks.
  • Sub-Zero: The master of ice, Sub-Zero uses freezing abilities to trap enemies and deliver powerful combos.
  • Raiden: The God of Thunder, Raiden uses his lightning abilities to control the battlefield and unleash devastating attacks.
  • Shang Tsung: The evil sorcerer and primary antagonist, capable of morphing into other fighters and utilizing their abilities.
  • Quan Chi: Another central villain in the Mortal Kombat series, known for his dark magic and ability to summon demons.

While the roster is smaller compared to traditional Mortal Kombat fighting games, it is appropriate for the action-adventure style of Shaolin Monks. The inclusion of both playable and non-playable characters allows for dynamic combat encounters and narrative progression, ensuring that players encounter a variety of unique fighters throughout their journey.

Conclusion: A Solid Action-Adventure Entry in the Mortal Kombat Series

Mortal Kombat - Shaolin Monks is an enjoyable and well-crafted action-adventure game that successfully blends the brutal combat of the Mortal Kombat series with a deeper, more narrative-driven experience. While the graphics and sound design are solid for the PS2 era, and the character roster is varied, the game’s true strength lies in its ability to offer a unique Mortal Kombat experience that departs from traditional fighting mechanics.
