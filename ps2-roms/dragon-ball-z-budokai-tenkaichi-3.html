Review of Dragon Ball Z - Budokai Tenkaichi 3 (PS2)

Released in 2007 by Namco Bandai, Dragon Ball Z - Budokai Tenkaichi 3 stands as one of the most celebrated entries in the Dragon Ball Z video game franchise. Developed by Spike and published for the PlayStation 2, this third installment in the Budokai Tenkaichi series takes the beloved anime’s intense battles and delivers an unforgettable experience with a mix of deep combat mechanics, a vast roster of characters, and stunning visuals. With a fast-paced battle system, a plethora of unlockables, and detailed fan-service elements, Budokai Tenkaichi 3 cemented itself as a fan-favorite and a standout title for the PS2. This review will explore the gameplay mechanics, character roster, graphics, sound, and legacy of the game while analyzing what makes it a truly exceptional entry in the Dragon Ball Z gaming universe.

Gameplay: Dynamic Combat and Deep Mechanics

Dragon Ball Z - Budokai Tenkaichi 3 excels in delivering one of the most exciting and visceral fighting experiences found in Dragon Ball Z video games. Unlike its predecessors, this installment introduced a unique 3D fighting system that places characters in a fully three-dimensional battlefield. The battles take place in expansive arenas, giving players the freedom to fly, jump, and dash around the arena to avoid attacks or set up devastating combos. This three-dimensional space allows for much more dynamic combat than traditional 2D fighters, and it’s a significant improvement over previous Budokai games.

The combat mechanics in Budokai Tenkaichi 3 are designed for both casual players and hardcore fighting game enthusiasts. The game allows players to unleash incredible attacks and combos, from simple punches and kicks to signature Dragon Ball Z moves like Kamehameha, Final Flash, and Spirit Bomb. The controls are relatively easy to pick up, but mastering the complex combination of dashes, blocks, and powerful moves requires precision and timing. This balance between accessibility and depth makes the game enjoyable for players of all skill levels.

One of the core features of the combat system is the Ki Gauge, which fills as players land attacks and take damage. Once the Ki Gauge is full, players can unleash devastating special attacks that not only do significant damage but also showcase some of the franchise’s most iconic moves. These attacks are beautifully animated and are sure to get any Dragon Ball Z fan excited. The game also introduced an innovative transformation mechanic, where players can switch between different power levels during battle. This means characters like Goku can transform into Super Saiyan forms, unlocking new abilities and increasing their combat power. These transformations give players an additional layer of strategy to consider during fights.

Another standout feature is the support mechanic. Characters can call upon allies to assist during a fight. This allows for team-based strategies and enhances the depth of combat. The AI-controlled support characters can provide helpful buffs or assist in dealing damage, giving players more ways to approach battles. The ability to fight on the ground, in the air, and with varied attack ranges adds variety to every battle, ensuring that no two fights ever feel the same.

Character Roster: A Dream Come True for Fans

One of the main attractions of Budokai Tenkaichi 3 is its massive character roster. With over 150 playable characters, the game offers an unparalleled selection of Dragon Ball Z characters, including every major hero and villain from the Dragon Ball Z, Dragon Ball GT, and Dragon Ball Z: Battle of Gods universes. From Goku and Vegeta to more obscure characters like Janemba and Turles, the game delivers an extensive variety of characters that fans of the series will appreciate.

What makes the roster even more impressive is the individual fighting style and movesets for each character. Every fighter feels distinct, with unique abilities, special moves, and transformations. For example, Goku’s fighting style revolves around energy blasts and physical punches, while characters like Trunks and Piccolo have their own set of signature attacks. Fans of the series will also appreciate the ability to play as transformed versions of characters. For example, Goku can transform into a Super Saiyan, and Vegeta can switch between his normal and Super Saiyan 2 forms. These transformations not only alter the character’s appearance but also provide unique fighting abilities that add layers of strategy to combat.

The game also features a large number of unlockable characters, giving players incentives to progress through the game’s various modes. Unlocking characters is done through a combination of completing story mode, battling in special tournaments, and collecting in-game items. The sheer number of characters ensures that players can always find someone to suit their preferred playstyle, and the inclusion of fan-favorite characters ensures that no matter how niche the player’s favorite fighter is, they will find them in the roster.

Graphics: Stunning Visuals for the PS2

For a PlayStation 2 title, Budokai Tenkaichi 3 offers some of the best graphics seen on the console. The character models are highly detailed and resemble their anime counterparts perfectly. The animations for each character’s special moves, from Goku’s Kamehameha to Frieza’s Death Ball, are fluid and true to the show. The special effects, particularly the energy attacks and explosive moves, are nothing short of spectacular. The developers did an excellent job of translating the high-energy action of Dragon Ball Z into the game’s visuals, capturing the vibrant, fast-paced, and action-packed essence of the series.

The arenas where the battles take place are similarly impressive, with dynamic backgrounds that change during combat. Some levels feature destructible environments, allowing players to smash through walls or blast the ground with their attacks. This adds a level of interactivity to the stages, making them feel more immersive. Whether you’re fighting on the arid surface of Planet Namek or in the ruins of a destroyed city, the environments are as visually appealing as the characters themselves.

The game runs smoothly, with very few noticeable frame rate dips during the action-packed fights. This is especially impressive given the scale of the battles, which often involve multiple fighters and massive energy blasts. The PS2 hardware was pushed to its limits to deliver a game that is both visually impressive and highly playable.

Sound: Authentic Voice Acting and Thrilling Soundtrack

Another area where Budokai Tenkaichi 3 excels is in its sound design. The game features a fully voiced cast, with the Japanese and English voice actors reprising their roles from the anime. The voice acting is top-notch, with each character’s lines delivered with the same energy and enthusiasm that made the Dragon Ball Z series so iconic. The voice acting enhances the experience, making players feel like they’re truly part of the Dragon Ball Z universe.

Additionally, the game’s soundtrack is a thrilling and energetic collection of music that perfectly complements the action on screen. The music swells during intense battles, and the fast-paced beats help keep the adrenaline high as you fight through opponents. The soundtrack features several tracks from the anime, as well as original compositions that further immerse players in the Dragon Ball Z world. The sound effects, from the explosive energy blasts to the heavy punches and kicks, also contribute to the game’s overall atmosphere.

Game Modes: A Comprehensive Experience

Dragon Ball Z - Budokai Tenkaichi 3 offers a variety of game modes, ensuring there is plenty of content to keep players engaged. The Story Mode lets players relive the events of the Dragon Ball Z saga, starting from the Saiyan Saga all the way through to the end of the Dragon Ball Z series. The mode follows the story closely, with major events and battles from the show faithfully recreated in the game. Fans of the anime will appreciate the attention to detail and the inclusion of some lesser-known story arcs from the series.

In addition to Story Mode, the game includes Tournament Mode, where players can compete against AI opponents or other players in a series of battles to win a championship. There is also an extensive Training Mode, which allows players to practice their moves and combos, perfecting their combat skills before heading into more difficult battles. The game’s multiplayer mode supports up to two players in local versus battles, where friends can face off in exciting one-on-one fights.

One of the more exciting features is the Dragon Adventure Mode, which lets players travel the Dragon Ball world and unlock hidden items, characters, and bonuses by completing various tasks. This mode offers a more exploratory and RPG-like experience compared to the more straightforward combat-focused modes.

Legacy: A Milestone in the Dragon Ball Z Video Game Franchise

Upon its release, Dragon Ball Z - Budokai Tenkaichi 3 received widespread praise for its comprehensive gameplay, vast character roster, and faithful representation of the Dragon Ball Z anime. The game is often regarded as one of the best Dragon Ball Z fighting games ever made, and its reputation has only grown over the years. Its unique 3D combat system, vast content, and memorable multiplayer modes made it a standout in the fighting game genre and a must-play for fans of the series.

Even years after its release, Budokai Tenkaichi 3 remains a beloved title among Dragon Ball Z fans, with many considering it the pinnacle of Dragon Ball Z video games. It succeeded in creating a game that captured the essence of the anime while providing a rich and engaging gameplay experience that was accessible to both casual and hardcore players. Its legacy is still felt today, as future Dragon Ball games continue to draw inspiration from the formula established by Budokai Tenkaichi 3.

Conclusion: A Must-Play for Dragon Ball Z Fans

Dragon Ball Z - Budokai Tenkaichi 3 is a comprehensive and thrilling fighting game that delivers an experience worthy of the Dragon Ball Z name. From its expansive character roster and deep combat mechanics to its stunning graphics and immersive sound, the game stands as one of the greatest Dragon Ball Z video games ever made. Whether you’re a long-time fan of the series or a newcomer to the Dragon Ball universe, Budokai Tenkaichi 3 offers an exciting and rewarding experience that is sure to keep you coming back for more. It’s a game that continues to be celebrated for its faithful recreation of the anime and its incredibly engaging gameplay, making it a must-play for any Dragon Ball Z fan.
