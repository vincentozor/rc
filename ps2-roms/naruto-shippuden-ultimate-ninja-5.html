Review of Naruto Shippuden - Ultimate Ninja 5 (PS2)

Released in 2007 for the PlayStation 2, Naruto Shippuden - Ultimate Ninja 5 is an action-packed fighting game that continues the beloved Naruto franchise in an exciting and engaging way. Developed by CyberConnect2 and published by Namco Bandai, the game is part of the Naruto: Ultimate Ninja series, which is known for delivering fast-paced, 2D arena battles featuring characters from the Naruto anime. In this review, we will explore the gameplay mechanics, story, character roster, graphics, sound design, and replayability of Naruto Shippuden - Ultimate Ninja 5, providing a thorough assessment of its merits and shortcomings.

Gameplay: Fast-Paced Combat and Strategy

The core of Naruto Shippuden - Ultimate Ninja 5 lies in its combat system. The game features 2D fighting mechanics where players control various characters from the Naruto universe and engage in fast-paced battles. The system is designed to be accessible for both newcomers and long-time fans of the series, featuring simple controls that allow players to perform a wide range of attacks, special moves, and combination techniques.

The game’s combat is centered around three key elements:

  • Basic Attacks: Players can perform light and heavy attacks, with the ability to chain them into combo strings. These attacks are essential for building up chakra, which is used for more powerful moves.
  • Chakra Abilities: Each character has unique chakra-based attacks that can deal heavy damage or provide strategic advantages during battle. Chakra can also be used to perform devastating jutsu techniques that are signature to each character.
  • Special Jutsu and Ultimate Jutsu: Players can unleash special jutsu moves that capture the essence of the Naruto anime. These range from individual attacks to team-based moves that involve multiple characters on the battlefield. The Ultimate Jutsu is the most powerful move a character can perform, often involving cinematic sequences that showcase the character’s abilities in a spectacular manner.

The combat in Ultimate Ninja 5 is designed to be fast and fluid, allowing for a seamless transition between basic attacks, chakra abilities, and ultimate moves. One of the highlights of the game is the use of team-based mechanics, where players can summon assist characters during the battle to help out in combat. This adds a layer of strategy, as players must choose the right time to call in these assists for maximum impact.

In addition to the main combat system, the game features several unique mechanics that add depth to the gameplay:

  • Team Battles: Unlike traditional one-on-one fights, Ultimate Ninja 5 allows for 3-on-3 team battles. Players can control one character while their team members support them with special moves or by providing chakra boosts.
  • Field Interaction: Certain stages feature interactive elements that players can use to their advantage, such as throwing objects or using the environment to block attacks.
  • Chakra Management: Managing chakra is crucial to success in the game. Players must balance their chakra usage for jutsu attacks and defensive maneuvers, as running out of chakra can leave characters vulnerable.

The gameplay is accessible yet offers a level of depth that will appeal to both casual players and competitive fans of the series. While the basic attacks and controls are straightforward, mastering the use of chakra, special jutsus, and team-based strategies requires practice and a keen understanding of each character's strengths and weaknesses.

Story: Following the Naruto Shippuden Arc

The story in Naruto Shippuden - Ultimate Ninja 5 is set during the Naruto Shippuden arc of the anime, focusing on Naruto Uzumaki’s quest to rescue his friend Sasuke Uchiha and the growing threats to the Hidden Leaf Village. The game follows the events of the anime, allowing players to relive key moments while also introducing some original content that ties into the Naruto lore.

The narrative is primarily conveyed through in-game cutscenes, which are drawn from the anime's art style. The story mode features a mix of battles, dialogue, and story progression, allowing players to engage in the plot while also participating in action sequences. Players will experience various story arcs from the anime, including Naruto’s pursuit of Sasuke, the battle against the Akatsuki, and the confrontation with powerful enemies like Orochimaru and Itachi Uchiha.

The story mode is divided into several chapters, each of which focuses on a specific set of characters or events from the Shippuden storyline. This progression ensures that players experience key moments from the anime while also being immersed in the game’s action-oriented gameplay. Although the plot closely follows the events of the anime, there are enough twists and original content to make it feel fresh for those already familiar with the source material.

Character Roster: A Wide Range of Fighters

One of the key attractions of Naruto Shippuden - Ultimate Ninja 5 is its expansive roster of playable characters. The game features over 40 characters, including fan-favorites from the Naruto universe such as Naruto Uzumaki, Sasuke Uchiha, Sakura Haruno, and Kakashi Hatake. Each character has their own unique fighting style, special moves, and jutsu abilities, making for varied and dynamic combat.

The character roster includes:

  • Naruto Uzumaki: The titular character, Naruto uses a combination of powerful taijutsu, shadow clones, and Rasengan techniques. His Ultimate Jutsu, Rasengan Barrage, is one of the most visually spectacular moves in the game.
  • Sasuke Uchiha: Sasuke’s fighting style revolves around his Sharingan and powerful ninjutsu abilities, including Chidori and Kirin. His agility and speed make him a formidable opponent.
  • Sakura Haruno: A strong support character who utilizes her superhuman strength, chakra control, and healing abilities. She can also summon powerful allies to assist in battle.
  • Kakashi Hatake: Known for his Sharingan and versatile techniques, Kakashi excels in both ranged and close-combat attacks. His Kamui technique can warp space and time, making him unpredictable in battle.
  • Gaara: The Kazekage of the Hidden Sand, Gaara uses sand-based attacks and defenses. His Ultimate Jutsu, Shukaku’s Sand Tsunami, can engulf the entire battlefield.
  • Orochimaru: A powerful rogue ninja with the ability to summon snakes and use forbidden jutsus. His diverse range of attacks makes him a difficult foe to defeat.

In addition to the main cast of characters, Ultimate Ninja 5 also includes a wide range of supporting and antagonist characters, such as the members of the Akatsuki, the Kage of various villages, and other notable ninjas from the Naruto universe. Each character has their own unique abilities, jutsus, and fighting styles, ensuring that players can experiment with different playstyles throughout the game.

Graphics: Capturing the Naruto Art Style

As with previous entries in the Ultimate Ninja series, Naruto Shippuden - Ultimate Ninja 5 features a cel-shaded art style that closely resembles the animation style of the Naruto anime. The game’s visuals are vibrant and colorful, with character models that faithfully capture the look of the anime’s characters. The backgrounds are well-designed, with each stage reflecting the environment from the anime’s world, from the Hidden Leaf Village to the desolate landscapes of the Land of Sound.

While the graphics may not be as polished as modern titles, the cel-shaded art style gives the game a timeless quality, making it visually appealing for fans of the anime. The character animations are smooth, and the special effects during combat, especially when performing Ultimate Jutsu, are eye-catching and dynamic. The cutscenes are well-executed, drawing directly from the anime’s iconic moments and presenting them in a manner that enhances the experience for players.

Sound: Music and Voice Acting

In keeping with the Naruto anime’s atmosphere, Ultimate Ninja 5 features a soundtrack that complements the action and drama of the game. The music is composed of energetic and emotional tracks that match the intensity of the battles and the pacing of the story. Whether in the middle of an epic showdown or during a tense moment of the narrative, the music helps to elevate the emotional impact of the game.

The game also features voice acting for the majority of the characters, with the English and Japanese voice cast reprising their roles. The voice acting is well done, with each character’s personality shining through their dialogue. Fans of the anime will appreciate the inclusion of voice work, as it adds authenticity to the experience. However, the game’s voice acting is not as extensive as the anime, so there are moments of silence during certain parts of the game.

Replayability: Challenges and Unlockables

While the main story mode in Naruto Shippuden - Ultimate Ninja 5 is engaging, the game also offers a variety of unlockables and challenges to keep players coming back. Players can unlock new characters, stages, and costumes by completing specific tasks or achievements within the game. There are also several extra modes, such as the Mission Mode, where players can take on side challenges to earn rewards and increase their character’s stats.

The game’s multiplayer mode allows players to battle against friends in head-to-head combat, which adds an extra layer of replayability. Additionally, the vast roster of characters ensures that players can continue to experiment with different combinations and strategies, enhancing the game’s longevity.

Conclusion: A Solid Entry for Naruto Fans

Naruto Shippuden - Ultimate Ninja 5 is an exciting and enjoyable entry in the Naruto franchise, combining fast-paced combat, a deep character roster, and a faithful representation of the anime’s story and world. While the game’s story mode is relatively short, the engaging combat, wide range of characters, and replayability ensure that Ultimate Ninja 5 remains a fun and satisfying experience for both casual players and Naruto fans alike. The game’s solid graphics, music, and voice acting further enhance the overall experience, making it one of the best action-fighting games based on the Naruto franchise available for the PlayStation 2.
