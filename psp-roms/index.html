PlayStation Portable Games

The PlayStation Portable (PSP), created by Sony Computer Entertainment, was one of the most innovative handheld gaming consoles of its time. Released in 2004 in Japan, and in 2005 in North America and the PAL regions, the PSP ushered in a new era for portable gaming. As part of the 7th generation of video game consoles, it offered impressive graphics and performance that rivaled 6th-generation consoles like the Xbox, PS2, and GameCube. The PlayStation Vita followed as its successor, but the PSP remains an iconic device in gaming history.

A Glimpse of the PSP Legacy

The PSP's dual processor and hardware accelerator enabled it to run ports from the PlayStation 2, offering incredible graphics and gameplay. While some critics initially questioned its price, the PSP was a tremendous success upon launch, garnering a loyal fanbase. Despite facing tough competition from the Nintendo DS, which outperformed it in sales, the PSP still achieved remarkable success and became one of the highest-selling handheld devices of all time.

With a vast library of games spanning various genres, the PSP earned a reputation for being home to some of the best RPGs and other action-packed titles.

PSP stands for PlayStation Portable, a handheld gaming console created by Sony.

The PSP was first released in December 2004 in Japan, followed by global releases in other regions. The handheld was officially discontinued in December 2014, after 10 successful years in the market.
