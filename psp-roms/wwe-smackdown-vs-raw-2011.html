WWE SmackDown Vs. RAW 2011 Review

The gameplay in WWE SmackDown Vs. RAW 2011 builds upon the foundations laid by its predecessors while introducing several new mechanics aimed at enhancing the wrestling experience. One of the most significant changes is the revamped control scheme that allows players to utilize the right analog stick for grapples. This shift makes grappling more intuitive but can lead to confusion for players accustomed to previous control systems[1][3].

Players can now execute their finishing moves at any time during a match, provided they have built up their finisher meter. This change adds an exciting dynamic to matches, allowing for surprise finishes that can catch opponents off guard[3][4]. However, some players have noted that the one-button reversal system can make matches feel overly simplistic, as it allows for easy counters to grapples, potentially reducing strategic depth[2][3].

Game Modes

WWE SmackDown Vs. RAW 2011 features several game modes that cater to different playstyles and preferences. The most notable modes include:

  • Road to WrestleMania: This story-driven mode allows players to experience a narrative arc with their chosen superstar. While it offers a variety of storylines and character interactions, some players feel that it lacks depth and can become repetitive over time[1][4].
  • WWE Universe Mode: This mode provides a sandbox experience where players can create their own rivalries and storylines. Although it offers a lot of customization options, some users have criticized it for feeling unpolished and lacking meaningful impact on the overall gameplay[3][5].
  • Exhibition Matches: Players can engage in quick matches against AI or friends, choosing from various match types such as Hell in a Cell, TLC (Tables, Ladders, and Chairs), and more. These matches are often where the game shines, providing fast-paced action and entertainment[4][5].

Graphics and Presentation

The graphics in WWE SmackDown Vs. RAW 2011 show improvement over previous titles but still have their shortcomings. Character models are generally well-designed, capturing the likenesses of popular WWE superstars effectively. However, some textures appear dated, particularly in crowd animations and backstage environments, which can detract from immersion during matches[1][2].

Animations are fluid and contribute positively to the overall presentation of the game. Signature moves and finishing maneuvers are executed with a satisfying level of realism that enhances the excitement of matches[4]. However, some players have reported issues with collision detection during certain moves, leading to awkward moments during gameplay[5].

User Experience and Accessibility

For new players or those unfamiliar with wrestling games, WWE SmackDown Vs. RAW 2011 may present a learning curve due to its extensive menu systems and options for customization. While veterans of the series may appreciate the depth available in creating custom wrestlers and storylines, newcomers might find it overwhelming at first[1][3].

The multiplayer aspect of WWE SmackDown Vs. RAW 2011 is where the game truly shines.
