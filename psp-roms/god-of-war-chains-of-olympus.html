God Of War - Chains Of Olympus Review

'God Of War - Chains Of Olympus' is a critically acclaimed action-adventure game developed by Ready at Dawn and published by Sony Computer Entertainment. Released for the PlayStation Portable (PSP) in 2008, it serves as a prequel to the original 'God of War' game. The game follows the story of Kratos, a Spartan warrior, as he embarks on a quest to save the world from impending doom.

Storyline

The storyline of 'Chains Of Olympus' is set before the events of the original 'God of War' game. Kratos, the protagonist, is tasked by the gods to find the missing sun god, Helios, and restore light to the world. The journey takes Kratos through various mythological realms, including the Underworld, where he faces numerous challenges and battles mythical creatures. The narrative is rich with Greek mythology and provides a deeper understanding of Kratos' character and his motivations.

Gameplay

The gameplay of 'Chains Of Olympus' is a blend of action, combat, and puzzle-solving. Players control Kratos as he battles enemies using a combination of melee attacks, magic, and quick-time events. The combat system is fluid and responsive, allowing players to execute powerful combos and special moves. The game also features platforming elements and environmental puzzles that require players to think strategically to progress.

Combat System

The combat system in 'Chains Of Olympus' is one of its standout features. Kratos wields the Blades of Chaos, which are chained weapons that can be used for both close and ranged attacks. Players can upgrade their weapons and abilities by collecting red orbs, which are earned by defeating enemies and solving puzzles. The game also introduces new magic abilities, such as the Efreet and the Light of Dawn, which add variety to the combat.

Boss Battles

Boss battles in 'Chains Of Olympus' are epic and challenging. Each boss has unique attack patterns and requires players to use different strategies to defeat them. The battles are visually impressive and often involve quick-time events that add a cinematic feel to the encounters. Some of the notable bosses include Charon, the ferryman of the Underworld, and Persephone, the queen of the Underworld.

Graphics and Sound

'Chains Of Olympus' is widely praised for its impressive graphics and sound design. The game pushes the technical capabilities of the PSP, delivering detailed character models, stunning environments, and smooth animations. The art direction is inspired by Greek mythology, with beautifully designed levels that capture the essence of ancient Greece.

Visuals

The visuals in 'Chains Of Olympus' are some of the best on the PSP. The game features high-quality textures, dynamic lighting, and intricate character designs. The environments are diverse, ranging from the sunlit city of Attica to the dark and eerie Underworld. The attention to detail is evident in every aspect of the game, making it a visually captivating experience.

Soundtrack

The soundtrack of 'Chains Of Olympus' is composed by Gerard Marino, who also worked on the previous 'God of War' games. The music is epic and orchestral, perfectly complementing the game's intense action and dramatic moments. The voice acting is also top-notch, with Terrence C. Carson reprising his role as Kratos and delivering a powerful performance.

Replay Value

'Chains Of Olympus' offers a decent amount of replay value. The game includes multiple difficulty levels, which provide a greater challenge for experienced players. There are also unlockable costumes and bonus content that can be accessed by completing the game. Additionally, players can revisit previous levels to find hidden treasures and collectibles.

Conclusion

'God Of War - Chains Of Olympus' is a must-play for fans of the series and action-adventure enthusiasts. The game delivers a compelling storyline, engaging gameplay, and stunning visuals that make it one of the best titles on the PSP. Whether you're a longtime fan of Kratos or new to the 'God of War' franchise, 'Chains Of Olympus' is an unforgettable gaming experience.

Final Thoughts

In conclusion, 'God Of War - Chains Of Olympus' is a testament to the quality and creativity of the 'God of War' series. It successfully brings the epic scale and intense action of the console games to the handheld platform, providing an immersive and enjoyable experience. With its captivating story, polished gameplay, and impressive presentation, 'Chains Of Olympus' stands out as one of the best games on the PSP.
